The full directory launches in 2026. Here’s exactly what you’ll be able to do with it — whether you’re planning an offsite or you own a venue.

If you plan retreats

  • Browse by state and market — the whole U.S. map, including the small markets other platforms skip.
  • Filter on what actually matters — group capacity, on-site lodging, full-property buyouts, meeting space, wellness and outdoor programs.
  • Trust the photos — every photo set is machine-verified against the property it claims to show before you ever see it.
  • Get straight answers on price — where a venue publishes rates, the register shows them; where it quotes per group, the listing says quote only instead of printing a made-up number.
  • Go direct — every listing links straight to the venue. The register is a reference work, not a middleman taking a cut of your booking.

Venue owners: claim yours early

  • Claiming is free — and the launch list goes first. After launch, venues can claim their listing to add details, photos, and current contact information. Owners on the launch list get first access, before the public.
  • Placement can’t be bought. Claiming improves your listing’s completeness — it never buys position. Ranking is earned through verification, for every venue, forever.
  • Corrections are fast. Wrong phone, old photos, new ownership? One message fixes it.

Why sign up before launch?

Because the launch email is the whole marketing plan. No drip campaigns, no “last chance” countdowns — one email when the doors open, sent to the people who asked for it. Planners on the list see the register before anyone else; venue owners on the list get first access to claiming.

Until then, the register keeps growing — the updates page tracks verification progress with real numbers, and the coverage page shows exactly where venues stand state by state.
